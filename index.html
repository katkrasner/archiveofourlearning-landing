How it started

A familiar story

I've been reading fics in English for years, telling everyone I'm doing it to learn the language. I'd jot down unfamiliar words and cool phrases — almost never returning to them.

Then IELTS happened. So I built a whole Chrome extension to organize my notes.

How it works

Four steps. Zero guilt.

You were going to read that 200k enemies-to-lovers fic anyway. Might as well learn something.

01

Highlight

See a word you don't know? Select it. Don't pretend you understood it from context this time.

02

Translate

Get instant translation — not just the dictionary entry, but what the word actually means in that sentence. Yes, even in smut.

03

Save

One click — the word is saved with the sentence, fic, author, fandom, and pairing. So you'll remember exactly where you were at 3am.

04

Remember

SM-2 spaced repetition brings words back at the perfect moment. Like that fic you swore you'd never reread.

Why does my translation look weird? Because it's not a dumb dictionary lookup. It translates the word as it's used in the actual sentence. Sometimes that means the result is surprising — that's the feature, not the bug. And if you hate it, just edit it. We're not your teacher.

Features

More features than your excuse needs

📖

Context-Aware Dictionary

Every word is saved with the sentence, the fic, the author, and the pairing. You'll know exactly which slow burn taught you "trembling".

🧠

Flashcards & Quiz Mode

Flip cards, quizzes, keyboard shortcuts. Spaced repetition (SM-2) — the same algorithm as Anki, but you don't have to make the cards yourself.

📊

Text Difficulty Badge

Know if a fic is above your level before you commit to 50 chapters. Or ignore the badge. We're not your mom.

✨

Word Highlighting

Your saved words glow on AO3 pages. Red = still learning. Green = nailed it. It's like your fic is giving you a progress report.

Fandom Achievements

Unlock trophies tied to pairings, fandoms, and ratings. Finally, tangible proof of how you spent your weekend.

Stats Dashboard

Words saved. Words learned. Fics devoured. Authors stalked. Study time. All the numbers you never asked for but secretly love.
