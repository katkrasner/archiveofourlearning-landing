How it started

A familiar story

I've been reading fics in English for years, telling everyone I'm doing it to learn the language. I'd jot down unfamiliar words and cool phrases — almost never returning to them.

Then IELTS happened. So I built a whole Chrome extension to organize my notes.

And got a little carried away.

It's called Archive of Our Learning because I thought I was being clever.

How it works

Four steps. Zero effort.

Your reading habit is already there. We just added the learning part.

01

Highlight

Select any word while reading a fic on AO3. That's it.

02

Translate

Get instant translation — not just the word, but the word in context of the sentence.

03

Save

One click — and the word is saved with the sentence, fic title, author, fandom, and pairing.

04

Remember

Flashcards and quizzes with SM-2 spaced repetition bring words back at the right time.

About context-aware translation: The translation captures the meaning of the word within the whole sentence, not just a dictionary definition. Sometimes the result might look non-obvious — that's because it reflects how the word is actually used. If you don't like it, you can always edit it manually.

Features

Everything you need. Nothing you don't.

Context-Aware Dictionary

Every word is saved with the sentence you found it in, the fic, the author, the fandom, and the pairing. Because context is everything.

Stats Dashboard
